Overview

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and often called Earth's twin due to its similar size and structure. However, its surface conditions are vastly different and extremely hostile to life as we know it.

Quick Facts

  • Distance from Sun: 108.2 million km (67.2 million miles)
  • Diameter: 12,104 km (7,521 miles)

Unique Characteristics

Venus rotates from east to west, opposite to most other planets. Its atmosphere traps heat through a powerful greenhouse effect, making it hotter than Mercury despite being farther from the Sun.

The surface is dry and barren, marked with volcanic plains, huge mountains like Maxwell Montes, and vast lava flows.
